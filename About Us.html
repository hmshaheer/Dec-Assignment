Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Popular types include canvas prints, framed prints, metal art, posters, and original paintings. Abstract art, nature scenes, and photography are also trending.

Consider the wall space you have available. A general rule is to aim for 60-75% of the wall space for larger pieces or a cohesive gallery wall. It's helpful to measure the area before shopping.

Hang art at eye level, typically 57-60 inches from the floor. Use appropriate hardware based on the weight of the piece, and consider using a level to ensure it's straight.

Start by laying out your pieces on the floor to find a pleasing arrangement. Mix different sizes and styles for visual interest, and use consistent framing or color themes to tie it all together.

Keep artwork out of direct sunlight to prevent fading, dust regularly with a soft cloth, and avoid using harsh chemicals. For framed pieces, check the back for any signs of damage or moisture periodically.
